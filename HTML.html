Alan Mathison Turing

“Sometimes it is the people no one can imagine anything of who do the things no one can imagine.”


Timeline of the life of Alan Turing

1912: Born in Maida Vale, London.

1922 - 1926: Educated at Hazelhurst Preparatory School.

1926: He went to Sherborne School.

1930: His friend and inspiration Christopher Collan Morcom dies from complications of bovine tuberculosis.

1931 - 1934: Turing studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge, where he was awarded first-class honours in mathematics.

1939: He solved the essential part of the naval indicator system, which was more complex than the indicator systems used by the other services.

1946: Turing was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) by King George VI for his wartime services, but his work remained secret for many years.

1946: He presented a paper which was the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.

1949: He became Deputy Director of the Computing Machine Laboratory, where he worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers, the Manchester Mark 1.

1949: Turing addressed the problem of artificial intelligence, and proposed an experiment that became known as the Turing test, an attempt to define a standard for a machine to be called "intelligent".

1954: Dies at the age of 41.
